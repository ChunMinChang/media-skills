### Theme 1 — Browser Detection: "Firefox Not Supported" Walls

**Signal: CRITICAL** | Breadcrumbs: 35+ | Meta-bugs: [1886128](https://bugzilla.mozilla.org/show_bug.cgi?id=1886128), [1886129](https://bugzilla.mozilla.org/show_bug.cgi?id=1886129)

**User-facing impact:** Users attempting to access a wide range of websites — gaming, financial services, government portals, enterprise tools, healthcare, and streaming — are greeted with error pages like "Firefox is not a supported browser," "Device or browser not supported," or "The site is incompatible with your browser." The site works in Chrome. The user has no recourse other than switching browsers.

This is the dominant theme in the webcompat bug queue. Two Knowledge Base tracking bugs organize the work:

- **[1886128](https://bugzilla.mozilla.org/show_bug.cgi?id=1886128) "Unsupported Browser"** — 250+ blocked bugs (Knowledge Base, NEW, no assignee). Tracks cases where sites explicitly gate on Firefox. Depends on [2022970](https://bugzilla.mozilla.org/show_bug.cgi?id=2022970) and [1972963](https://bugzilla.mozilla.org/show_bug.cgi?id=1972963).
- **[1886129](https://bugzilla.mozilla.org/show_bug.cgi?id=1886129) "Unsupported browser warning"** — 200+ blocked bugs (Knowledge Base, NEW, no assignee). Tracks softer warnings that permit but degrade the experience.

**Root causes are diverse.** The underlying `depends_on` of 1886128 reveal that many "unsupported" blocks trace to missing Firefox features:
- [1749532](https://bugzilla.mozilla.org/show_bug.cgi?id=1749532) — `MediaStreamTrackProcessor` not yet shipped (blocking GeForce Now, 1898998 and others)
- [1829401](https://bugzilla.mozilla.org/show_bug.cgi?id=1829401) — Pointer lock `unadjustedMovement` support missing
- [1987671](https://bugzilla.mozilla.org/show_bug.cgi?id=1987671) — `pointerrawupdate` event did not set `movementX/Y` (now RESOLVED)

**Site-patch mitigation is active.** The webcompat team applies UA overrides and site-specific patches as stopgaps (keyword `webcompat:sitepatch-applied`). Thomas Wisniewski [:twisniewski] owns the largest share of these.

### Theme 4 — Page Load Timing: setTimeout / Navigation Regressions

**Signal: MEDIUM-HIGH** | Breadcrumbs: 6 | Platform bugs: [2017786](https://bugzilla.mozilla.org/show_bug.cgi?id=2017786), [1922677](https://bugzilla.mozilla.org/show_bug.cgi?id=1922677)

**User-facing impact:** Pages fail to load on first visit, show a blank or wrong content (background image instead of login form), require multiple hard refreshes to render, or intermittently fail to display dynamic content. These are timing-sensitive failures related to how Firefox handles `setTimeout` during the page load lifecycle.

**[2017786](https://bugzilla.mozilla.org/show_bug.cgi?id=2017786)** "Set dom.timeout.defer_during_load = false" (Core::DOM, NEW, no assignee) — Firefox defers `setTimeout(..., 0)` calls during page load, breaking sites that depend on immediate setTimeout execution at load time. A platform fix is in progress:

### Suggestions for Improving This Skill

1. **Add a `webcompat:ua-sniffing` keyword filter** as its own expansion query — this keyword appears on several Theme 1 bugs and would surface them more reliably than dep-crawl alone.
2. **Add a `webcompat:site-wait` status check** — several Akamai bugs have this keyword, indicating they are waiting on the vendor, and surfacing wait-time alongside them would add useful prioritization context.
3. **Consider a secondary date-range query** specifically for `creation_time` within the window ordered by `creation_time ASC` to ensure early-window bugs (Oct–Nov) are not starved by the `changeddate DESC` ordering.
4. **The cc_count field is only available to authenticated Bugzilla users.** The skill should note this as a known gap and suggest using `include_fields=cc` and measuring array length as a fallback.
5. **A `webcompat:sitepatch-applied` count** across themes would be a useful signal — it indicates active triage investment and sites where a platform fix would eliminate the site-specific overhead.
